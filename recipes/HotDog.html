HotDog

The hot dog is a food in the form of a sandwich that is generated with the combination of a boiled Frankfurt or Viennese sausage.

Hot dogs come in all shapes and sizes! From the classic all-beef frankfurter to regional specialties like the Chicago-style dog (dragged through the garden!), there's a hot dog out there for everyone. Hot dogs are endlessly customizable with a variety of toppings and condiments. Ketchup, mustard, and relish are the classics, but you can get adventurous with sauerkraut, kimchi, sriracha, and even pineapple (if you dare!).

Ingredients

  • Meat: This is usually a combination of trimmings from various cuts of beef, pork, or even poultry. Higher quality hot dogs will list specific cuts of meat, like beef chuck.
  • Seasonings: This is where the magic happens! Salt, pepper, garlic, onion, and paprika are common, but other spices can be included depending on the brand's recipe.
  • Preservatives: These help to extend the shelf life of hot dogs. Sodium nitrite is a common one, but there may be others
  • Fillers: Some hot dogs may include fillers, like breadcrumbs or cereal, to extend the volume or reduce cost.

Steps

  1. Heat the panels in steam or on an iron for a while, preventing them from browning.
  1. Heat the oil over medium heat adding the sausages to a frying pan, turning them so that they brown on all sides.
  1. Prepare the pink sauce by combining mayonnaise, ketchup, mustard, lemon juice and soy.
  1. Place a sausage on each bun, add a little lettuce, a little tomatoes, onion and then continue with the sauce.
  1. Lastly (optional) add a little avocado and grated cheese.
