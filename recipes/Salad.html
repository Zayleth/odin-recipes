Salad

A Caesar salad is a salad of romaine lettuce, chopped chicken breast, and croûtons with lemon juice, olive oil, egg, Worcestershire sauce, anchovies, garlic, Dijon mustard, Parmesan cheese, and black pepper.

In Mexican restaurants this salad is generally prepared for two people, in front of the diners; although the recipes vary; The usual thing is to spread the salad bowl with garlic and prepare the dressing there with anchovies, raw egg yolks, lemon juice, salt, pepper, seasoning juice, Worcestershire sauce and, sometimes, mustard. Whole leaves of dried apricot lettuce are mixed with this dressing, then served on the plate arranged lengthwise, garnished with Melba-type croutons and sprinkled with Parmesan cheese. Although the ingredients and quantities vary, the Caesar salad service does not present major changes.

Ingredients

  • 1 large lettuce
  • 1 grilled chicken breast cut into strips (optional)
  • ½ cup sliced ​​or coarsely grated Parmesan cheese
  • ½ cup of bread cubes (baguette, ciabatta, marraqueta or whatever bread you have at home)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon paprika paprika
  • 1 extra large egg yolk
  • 2 teaspoons Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons of vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon lemon
  • ½ teaspoon Garlic Powder

Steps

  1. For the dressing, put all the ingredients except the oil in a blender. Blend until you have a homogeneous mixture. With the blender running at minimum, add the olive oil little by little to have an emulsified and thick mixture.
  2. Season with gourmet sea salt and gourmet ground white pepper.
  3. Heat the oven to 200°C.
  4. For the croutons: Mix the bread with the olive oil and paprika. Put the bread in a baking tin (in a single layer). Bake for 3 to 5 minutes or until bread is crisp and golden.
  5. Put the lettuce on a plate, drizzle with the dressing. Decorate with chicken, croutons and parmesan cheese. Serve immediately.
